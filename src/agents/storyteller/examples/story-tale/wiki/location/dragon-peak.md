# Dragon's Peak

**Location:** Mountain range approximately thirty miles east of Emberhold village, the highest chain in the region, elevation peaking at roughly 14,000 feet at the Obsidian Crown
**Terrain:** Mix of exposed granite ridge, scree slopes, high meadow, and a system of natural caves that extend deep into the mountain's core. The caves connect at their deepest point to a chamber that predates human settlement in the region — dragon-carved entrances show a script that modern scholars have never been able to read.
**Climate:** Extreme diurnal variation. Summer days can exceed 25 degrees in the shelter of the crags; winter temperatures at the Crown regularly fall below minus thirty. Snow persists on the north faces year-round.

## Historical Significance

Dragon's Peak was the last major nesting site in the region before the Concord wars. Before that, it served as a meeting ground between human and dragon delegations, hence the carved chambers at the cave entrances. The highest chamber — the Atrium — contains a rough fresco of two figures, one scaled and one not, standing side by side against a backdrop of what appears to be a star map.

## Modern Status

After the events of the story, the Peak was designated a protected site under the terms of the Emberhold Treaty. The Council agreed not to approach within five miles of the cave entrances. Kael visits twice a year.
