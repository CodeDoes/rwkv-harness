# Lyra

**Role:** Bronze dragon, last of her kind
**Age:** 427 years (estimation based on scale growth patterns)
**Physical Description:** Bronze scales the colour of autumn oak, gold eyes with vertical slit pupils, wingspan approximately thirty feet when fully extended, a scar along her left flank from a Council ash-magic strike. She walks with a slight favour toward her rear left leg — an old injury from an earlier skirmish that never healed completely.
**Abilities:** Fire breath (suppressed by the Alloy Collar Kael made), night vision, flight, a form of empathetic perception that allows her to detect fear and deception at short range. She also remembers the old dragon-songs — melodic structures encoded in breath and rumble that have a measurable effect on human emotional states.

## Backstory

Lyra was hatched in the dragon-carved halls beneath Dragon's Peak in the final year of the old concord, when humans and dragons still traded and travelled together. Her clutch-mates were killed over the following decades by Council hunters using ash-magic — a substance derived from volcanic glass that burns through dragon-scale and lingers in wounds, preventing proper healing. By the time she reached the high meadows alone at around 350 years of age, Lyra had already buried three of her own offspring.

She avoided Emberhold and human settlements for decades after, living on the high crags where the air was cold enough to keep the wounds in her flank from festering badly. The encounter with Kael was not an accident in the literal sense — she had been moving downhill, slower than she should have been, because she had stopped believing there was any reason to flee.

## Relationship with Kael

The bond formed in weeks rather than months because both of them were genuinely starving for something they couldn't name. Kael, whose life had been the repetition of maintaining other people's tools and objects, found in Lyra a presence that asked nothing of him other than what he could freely give. Lyra, who had lost every dragon relationship she'd ever had to Council violence, was cautious but not closed — Kael's patience had a quality she hadn't encountered in a human in centuries.
