# The Ashen Council

**Type:** Political-military organisation with religious framing
**Founded:** Circa 180, formally reorganised under the current charter in 234
**Headquarters:** The Ivory Tower, capital city — a black-stone spire that was originally a temple to an older, unrelated deity. The Tower's upper chambers contain classified research archives on dragon biology, ash-magic formulas, and the full history of the Council's anti-dragon campaigns.
**Leader:** Councillor Maren, a former scholar of comparative law who rose to leadership after the previous High Councillor was assassinated in 312. She is competent, ruthless in pursuit of stated objectives, and privately uncertain about whether those objectives are still the right ones.
**Strength:** Estimated 200+ active operatives, divided into three field arms: Hunters (direct engagement with dragons), Archivists (intelligence and historical research), and Wardens (urban enforcement and public messaging).

## Official Position

The Council describes dragons as "structural predators" — beings whose natural behaviours (territoriality, size, fire breath) make coexistence with organised human settlement impossible. Their primary stated objective is the removal of all free-roaming dragons from human-inhabited territory and their containment in designated reserves. This is a generous reading of their actual charter, which in its unredacted form specifies extermination rather than containment.

## Methods

- **Ash-magic:** A compound derived from crushed obsidian and a binder extracted from rare alpine lichens. It burns through dragon-scale at close range and leaves lingering thermal damage that slows healing. The recipe is closely held; only four Archivists know the full formula.
- **Tracking:** Enchanted compasses calibrated to dragon blood residue rather than magnetic north. Effective within approximately five miles.
- **Propaganda:** A sustained campaign in human-populated areas framing dragons as existential threats, largely successful due to the absence of any surviving large-scale human-dragon cooperation for centuries.

## Motives

The Council genuinely believes it has the right and the duty to remove dragons from the human world. Over time, that belief hardened into institutional identity — the organisation now cannot easily disavow its own founding premise, regardless of whether its leadership individually wishes to. Maren, in particular, has shown signs of doubt; whether she will act on them is the unresolved question that drives the final act.
