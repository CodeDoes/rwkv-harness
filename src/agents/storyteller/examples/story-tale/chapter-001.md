# Chapter 1: The Discovery

The forge fire hissed as Kael plunged the red-hot steel into the water trough. Steam billowed upward, carrying the sharp smell of quenching oil and burnt sweat. He'd been at the anvil since before dawn, heating and folding and heating again — a young blacksmith's work was never done, not in a village like Emberhold that depended on one forge for everything from horseshoes to gate hinges.

A shadow crossed the window. Kael looked up and saw nothing but the familiar sway of oak branches in the wind. He resumed his work, but the rhythm had broken. The fire was too bright, the qenching too loud. Then he heard it: a low, rumbling moan that seemed to shake the very ground beneath his feet, more felt than heard, the kind of sound that travels through bone rather than air.

He grabbed his lantern and stepped outside. The night was clear and cold, the mountain road a ribbon of packed earth winding upwards toward Dragon's Peak. The sound grew louder as he climbed, and with it came a faint orange glow, not firelight but the last of the day's warmth reflecting from something enormous at the ridge. Kael crested the final rise and froze.

A massive creature lay crumpled in the ravine, bronze scales cracked and oozing in places where they had been rent by something hard and fast. One eye opened slowly — amber, slit-pupilled, ancient beyond any measure Kael could name — and fixed him with a gaze that was both fierce and pleading. The dragon inhaled, a ragged, wet sound. "Help me," she breathed, and her voice was smaller than he expected, more tired. Kael did not run. He knelt.
