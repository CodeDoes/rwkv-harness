# Chapter 3: The Sacrifice

The Ashen Council arrived at dawn. Three figures in grey cloaks stood at Emberhold's wooden gate, flanked by a dozen soldiers in burnished armour. Kael met them at the gate itself, pretending he had been up early to unlock it. The lead councillor — a woman named Maren with a voice like stone grinding on glass — asked him directly whether he knew of a dragon sheltering in the high meadows. Kael said no. Maren did not believe him.

She gave the village twenty-four hours. After that, the Council would burn the high passes area by area until they found Lyra. Kael returned to the forge and set to work — not on the Council's deadline but on something he'd been crafting for three weeks in secret. He worked through the day and into the night, the forge fire his only companion, shaping a collar of iron-gold alloy that would dampen Lyra's fire breath enough for her to be moved without harming the Council's soldiers.

Lyra refused it when she saw it. "I won't be taken," she said flatly. "Even restrained, I'd fight. I'd die before they got me to the Ivory Tower." Kael argued. She argued back. In the end, neither had their way. Before dawn on the final morning, Lyra left the cave on her own terms. She flew toward the Council's camp with her wings spread, roaring so loud the valley shook. It was not surrender. It was not a fight. It was a choice to be seen, to be acknowledged as a person rather than a specimen. She gave herself up to buy Kael — and Emberhold — time. What she bought, ultimately, was hope.
