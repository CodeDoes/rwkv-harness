# Mara

**Role:** Professional thief, later the unwilling steward of the Shadow Box
**Age:** Around 24 at the time of the Heist
**Origin:** Lower Ward, Duskfall City — raised by a fencesmith after her parents disappeared
**Abilities:** Exceptional spatial memory, lockpicking, parkour across dense urban rooftops, a preternatural sense for when something's watching her from dark places

## Background

Mara grew up in the cramped lanes of the Lower Ward, where survival meant knowing where to stand and when to move. She took to thieving not from greed but because locks were honest — they told you when you'd failed and gave you another chance to try again. People, she found, were less predictable.

The Shadow Box changed that calculus entirely. After stealing it from the Grand Archive, Mara discovered she could no longer separate her own shadow from the shadows in the room with her. They shared edges. They responded to the same stimuli. Over the weeks that followed, she learned to step into darkness and emerge from another — a trick that made her both more capable and more vulnerable than she'd ever been.

## Key Moments

- The Heist: lifting the Shadow Box from the Archive's western vault, acting on a fence-client's favour rather than money.
- The realisation: watching the shadows in her room pool and reorganise overnight, forming shapes that looked almost like handwriting.
- The Bargain: walking into the Umbral Order's cliffside cathedral to reopen the city's buried history rather than seal it shut again.
- The choice: keeping the Box's weight, accepting that she would never again stand in sunlight without carrying its equivalent in darkness.
