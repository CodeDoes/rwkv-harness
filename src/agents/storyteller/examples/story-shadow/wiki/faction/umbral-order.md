# The Umbral Order

**Type:** Semi-secret civic authority with religious overtones
**Founded:** Circa 274 (post-Calamity), publicly acknowledged only after the Spreading
**Symbol:** A black sun behind a veiled eye
**Leadership:** High Confessor Valera, seated in the Cathedral beneath the cliff face
**Known Strength:** Approximately 120 active operatives, plus an unknown number of embedded agents in the guild councils

## Official Mandate

The Order describes itself as the custodian of "what was sealed." Their public charter, released after the Spreading began, authorised them to impose martial law, restrict movement between districts, and appropriate "objects of civic danger." In practice this meant controlling access to the Shadow Box's release site, managing the official narrative, and conducting a quiet purge of anyone who had been inside the Archive on the night of the Heist.

## Real Motives

The Order's private objectives, revealed in fragments over subsequent weeks, centre on controlling the Unbinding rather than stopping it. The city was built atop something older than its laws, and the Order had spent three centuries keeping it sealed. Mara's theft of the Shadow Box was not the disaster the Order claimed — it was an unwitting correction of the forced status quo.

## Methods

- Blue-flame lanterns that suppress shadow-movement in a ten-yard radius.
- Curfew enforcement teams trained to move silently through ambient darkness.
- A network of safe houses and supply caches maintained since before the city's official founding.

## Fate

After the Bargain, the Order's cathedral was partially collapsed. High Confessor Valera survived and, according to Mara's later account, negotiated a truce: the Order would stand down in exchange for Mara's guarantee that the sealed content beneath the city would not be released unconditionally.
