# Duskfall City

**Location:** Eastern coastal city-state, built into and beneath a volcanic cliff face
**Population:** Approximately 80,000 at the time of the Heist, declining rapidly as shadows spread
**Government:** Martial law imposed by the Umbral Order; prior governance was a council of guild representatives
**Architecture:** Multi-layered — the Upper City of glass and iron, the Lower Ward's packed-timber warren, the submerged district (formerly a harbour, now silted) accessible only at low tide

## The Grand Archive

The city's central repository of knowledge was housed in a black-iron dome in the Upper City's civic quarter. It held three centuries of tax records, trade charts, legal precedents, and — in its deepest vault — the Shadow Box. The Archive's wards were designed to contain dangerous artefacts, not merely protect them. The Box was released anyway.

## The Shadow Problem

Within days of the Heist, certain districts began experiencing permanent localised darkness that standard light sources could not penetrate. Lanterns guttered. Fires smothered. The shadows stretched independently of objects, arranging themselves into recognisable shapes at night — mostly humanoid, mostly watching. The phenomenon was named "the Spreading" by city officials and "the Unbinding" by the Umbral Order.

## Geography

The city clings to a basalt cliff with the sea on one side and sheer rock on the other. Escape routes are limited and known. The Under-Cellar — a natural cave system beneath the Lower Ward — was sealed three centuries prior for reasons that the Archive's surviving records do not explain.
