# Chapter 3: The Bargain

The Umbral Order's fortress was a cathedral carved into the city's cliff face, its doors guarded by men who moved with a coordination that was almost inhuman. Mara went in alone, the Box in one hand and Kael's lantern in the other. The lantern was her only advantage — the Order's blue flame could be overpowered, Kael had shown her, but only at close range.

The High Confessor sat in a chamber beneath the altar, a woman whose face was half-shadow even in direct light. "You brought it back," she said, as though she'd been expecting Mara personally. "The Box chooses its bearer. You were always going to come." She explained what Mara had already begun to suspect: the shadows weren't a plague, they were a veil. Behind them waited the original architects of the city, sealed away three centuries ago. The Box was a key.

Mara stood at the crossroads. Return the Box to its vault and the shadows would retreat, but the city would remain sealed to its own history. Keep the Box and let the veil dissolve, risking chaos in the streets. She made her choice. The Bargain struck that night rewrote the city's future — and Mara's own. Some doors, once opened, cannot be closed.
