# Chapter 2: The Spreading

Mara woke on a rooftop in the Lower Ward, the Box burning cold against her ribs. She'd spent the night in the old templar watchtower, and even stone and iron hadn't kept the shadows out. They pooled in the corners of the room, leaking through mortar joints like water through cracked glass.

By noon, three people were missing. By dusk, five. Mara watched from the bridge as the Umbral Order's insignia appeared on every official notice — their black sun sigil stamped over district names, their soldiers patrolling in pairs with lanterns that burned blue rather than gold. The Order had been a conspiracy theory, a rumour whispered in tavern back rooms. Now they ran the city's response.

She found Kael in the under-market, a fencesmith who'd repaired her lockpicks a dozen times. "The Box feeds on concealment," he said, turning the obsidian lid in his hands. "Every shadow it touches grows heavier. Eventually there won't be light left to hide in." Mara looked at the spreading darkness on the district map and recognised the pattern. It wasn't random. It was reaching toward something — or somewhere.
