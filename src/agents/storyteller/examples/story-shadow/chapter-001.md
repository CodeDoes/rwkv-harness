# Chapter 1: The Heist

Mara had spent three years learning the streets of Duskfall City the way a musician learns a score — every alley, every roofline, every guard rotation. The Grand Archive sat at the city's heart, a brutalist dome of black iron and stained glass that seemed to absorb even the moonlight. Inside, behind twelve wards and a door that required three different keys turned in precise sequence, rested the Shadow Box.

She slipped through the side window at 2:17 AM, moving like smoke across the reading floor. The Box sat on a velvet pedestal in the western vault, exactly where the ledger said it would be. Mara's fingers brushed the cold obsidian surface. The moment she lifted it, she felt something uncoil in her chest — a pressure, like breathing underwater.

By the time the first alarm rang, she was three blocks away, the Box wrapped in oilcloth and tucked beneath her cloak. Behind her, the shadows in the Archive's corridors began to writhe. They stretched taller than the shelves, darker than the night outside. Duskfall City had guards and walls and a proud tradition of law. None of that mattered now. Something had been opened, and it was already learning the shape of the city.
