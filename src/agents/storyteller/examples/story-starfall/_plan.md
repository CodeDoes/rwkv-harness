# Starfall Saga: Plan

## Premise

When a meteorite carrying an extraterrestrial consciousness crashes in the Whispering Woods, astronomer Celeste Voss discovers she can communicate with the entity inside. As she learns to interpret a sprawling, non-local lattice of information running through the local environment, the Observatory Council moves to weaponise the discovery. Celeste must negotiate the terms of first contact with an institution that knows how to suppress it — and find a way to let the star's purpose complete.

## Structure

### Chapter 1: The Falling Star

Celeste is mapping alone at the high observatory when a highly anomalous object crashes in the woods below. She descends before authority can prevent her and finds a pulsing crystal embedded in glassified soil. This chapter establishes her character through her first, instinctive response to the extraordinary: not panic, analysis.

### Chapter 2: The Star-Speaker

Celeste spends weeks learning the entity's language — an overlay of contextual knowledge deposited directly into cognition. Meanwhile, the Observatory Council's Containment Division arrives and begins the organising paperwork that will lead to suppression. The chapter's tension grows as Celeste realises suppression will destroy the lattice's constructive regional effects.

### Chapter 3: The Ascension

The Council returns with force. Celeste meets them with an offer: she will broker the lattice's knowledge to human institutions, rather than the Council seizing it unilaterally. The chapter resolves her personal growth (she has become something neither human nor fully star-entity), the Council's institutional arc, and the wider question of what first contact should look like.
