# Celeste Voss

**Role:** Astronomer and first human Star-Speaker
**Age:** 34 at the time of the Starfall
**Professional Background:** eleven years at the Northern High Observatory, specialising in atmospheric optics and stellar spectroscopy. She was passed over for promotion three times in favour of candidates with better institutional connections. Colleagues described her as meticulous, obstinate, and unusually comfortable with long periods of solitude.
**Abilities:** Precise spatial reasoning, pattern recognition across large datasets, an intuitive grasp of non-local information structures (the lattice), increasing capacity to receive the star-entity's knowledge directly without mediation.

## Personal History

Celeste was raised in a town near the observatory by a carpenter father and a schoolteacher mother. Both are alive; neither fully understands what she does. She has no partner, no children, and a long list of colleagues who think she has gone slightly mad since the Starfall. She does not argue with them much. The lattice has ways of being right that do not require consensus.

The relationship with the star-entity — which she thinks of informally as "Stella" — began in the second week. It was a process of slow attunement: sitting quietly at the crater's edge, allowing the overlay of contextual knowledge to surface, tracking what arrived and what she could make of it. The first words in plain language came on the eighteenth day: *I am not your visitor.* After that, the conversations became more structured.

## Internal Conflict

Celeste carries genuine affection for the institution of the Observatory even as she distrusts its leadership. She wants what the lattice provides — a new way of understanding stars, weather, time — to reach as many people as possible, not just a containment division with a conflict-of-interest charter. Her solution, brokered to the Council in Chapter 3, reflects that compromise instinct: she will teach, not simply hand over.
