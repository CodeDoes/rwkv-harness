# Observatory Council

**Type:** Public research institution with classified operational divisions
**Founded:** 1902 (Observatory charter), Containment Division established 214 after a prior stellar impact event
**Primary Site:** Northern High Observatory complex — main dome, three secondary structures, and the lower vault. The vault contains seventeen classified objects recovered from prior impact events, all currently under containment review.
**Leadership:** Director Hargrove, a career administrator with a long record of prioritising institutional stability over scientific ambition. She is not opposed to what Celeste is doing — she is opposed to the risk of it.

## Containment Division

The Council's operational arm for non-standard events. It has a three-stage protocol: Observe, Classify, Contain. Suppression is classified as a Containment-stage action and requires Director-level authorisation. The Division's leadership — Head Warden Pike — is both personally ambitious and genuinely convinced that first-contact scenarios have historically ended badly for the contacted culture. He is not wrong; he is also not entirely right.

## Methods

- **Resonance coils:** Developed from protocols in the 214 Containment founding documents. They suppress lattice activity within a defined radius and, if left active beyond a threshold duration, induce dormancy in the core itself.
- **Classification requests:** Legal instruments that temporarily restrict researcher access to anomalous objects. In Celeste's case, the Council used a Class Three containment order — the highest level short of emergency confiscation.
- **Intelligence division:** Small, quietly funded, with access to movement data for individuals marked for observation. They had a profile on Celeste before the Starfall.

## Motivations

The Observatory Council is not a villainous institution. It is a human institution running on inherited fear dressed as caution, acting inside procedures that were written for a different problem and have not been re-examined since. Their primary fear is uncontrolled release of knowledge or capability that cannot be undone. They are not entirely wrong about this. They are just very wrong about who should make the decision.
